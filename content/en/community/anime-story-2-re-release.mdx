### Mastering Story Mode in Anime Story 2

Story mode is the foundation of your progression in *Anime Story 2*, providing crucial resources like XP, gold, and gems. To efficiently clear stages, especially with the impending *Anime Story 2 re-release*, unit selection is key.

One highly recommended unit for early to mid-game story progression is Ace. Ace's full AoE (Area of Effect) capabilities make him incredibly effective at clearing waves of enemies quickly. Players suggest aiming for a triple S rating on Ace's attack, crit range, or crit damage, depending on the trait. Achieving a triple S on stats like attack often requires around 6,000 to 6,500 takedowns, which is easily achievable with Ace due to his widespread damage. With a well-built Ace, you can potentially clear stages up to Soul Haven.

For later stages, especially the last map which offers the best all-in-one farm for XP, gold, and gems, units like Ten or Remu are highly effective. If you're fortunate enough to get Beiru from a secret orb (or by using codes), he can also significantly boost your story progression. Another viable option is Tempest, particularly for Nightmare stages, which can also yield excellent rewards.

A clever, albeit slightly buggy, method for rapid gem farming involves Act 3 Nightmare with double Genos. While it might not offer the best XP or gold, it's reportedly one of the fastest ways to acquire gems, completing stages in under 2.5 minutes. Keep an eye out for any changes to these farming spots once the *Anime Story 2 re-release* goes live.

### Conquering Raids for Valuable Loot

Raids are an essential part of endgame progression in *Anime Story 2*, offering unique rewards and challenging encounters. For new players, raids can seem daunting, but with the right unit compositions, they become much more manageable.

*   **Recommended Units for Raids:**
    *   **Broly (Rookie):** Despite not always having elemental advantage, Broly Rookie deals significant damage, making him a strong contender for raid teams.
    *   **Monarch:** If you're building a team for the Slime event, Monarch is a must-have and also performs well in general raids.
    *   **Eisen:** Often considered the best unit for farming raids, Eisen's ability to stun enemies is incredibly powerful. Pairing him with a unit like Beiru can make raid clears even smoother, especially if Eisen attacks first.
    *   **Beiru & Gojo:** This duo is effective, particularly because Gojo doubles damage over time effects. Beiru doesn't necessarily need Monarch for this setup.
    *   **High DPS Single-Target Units:** Generally, any unit with high single-target damage output will excel in raids.

Experimenting with your unit's stats, particularly focusing on damage or critical chance, can significantly impact your raid performance. For instance, while critical chance might seem appealing for Eisen, sometimes pure damage output with a good relic like a baseball bat proves more consistent for one-shotting bosses. The *Anime Story 2 re-release* might introduce new raid bosses or mechanics, so staying flexible with your team is key.

### Dominating the Slime Apocalypse Event

The Slime Apocalypse event in *Anime Story 2* demands a specific team composition for optimal farming and high scores. Community-tested strategies often involve unique unit synergies to achieve quick clears.

*   **Optimal Slime Apocalypse Team (Community-Tested):**
    *   **Shiny Ultimate Warrior:** A crucial unit that deals 2x more damage when attacking with elemental damage. Since the slimes in this event are water-based, this unit becomes incredibly powerful.
    *   **Bond:** Essential for one-shotting enemies below 25% HP, providing a swift finisher to the waves.
    *   **Cyborg (Level 193):** This is a unique pick. At level 193, Cyborg has just enough health and damage to die upon activation, detonating and dealing massive AoE damage to clear the slimes. Leveling him too high might prevent him from dying at the right moment, disrupting the strategy.
    *   **Tatsumiki:** Provides a significant 30% damage boost to the team.
    *   **Rookie:** Acts as a tank, absorbing attacks and stuns, allowing your damage dealers to operate effectively.

This team composition aims for rapid clear times, often within 9-12 seconds. Broly is also a strong opener, often one-shotting the initial wave. This efficient farming method can yield substantial amounts of currency, as demonstrated by players accumulating over 70,000 currency from consistent runs. The strategies for the Slime Apocalypse might evolve with the *Anime Story 2 re-release*, so keep an eye on community discussions for updated builds.

### Ascending the Tower: A Game of RNG and Strategy

The Tower in *Anime Story 2* is a challenging mode that heavily relies on RNG (Random Number Generation) and strategic unit placement. While new units from the *Anime Story 2 re-release* are expected to shake up the meta, certain units remain consistently valuable.

*   **Essential Tower Units:**
    *   **Eisen:** A must-have due to his ability to stun three enemies at the start of a round for one turn, and deal 200% damage on his first attack. This can often lead to one-shots against weaker enemies.
    *   **Itachi:** Provides a 25% chance to avoid damage and counterattack for 50% damage when hit. After being hit, the next attack against Itachi misses. This passive offers crucial survivability and damage.
    *   **Cyborg (Situational):** Controversial but potentially useful. If the enemy team has AoE units that don't instantly wipe your team, Cyborg can die and detonate, clearing the enemy. However, if your entire team is wiped, it's still a loss.
    *   **Sound Breather:** A high-risk, high-reward unit. If he lands his combo, he can devastate enemy teams, but his performance is highly dependent on RNG.
    *   **Slime:** Offers a 10% damage buff to your team and his black flames can make enemies take 10% more damage.
    *   **Beiru:** A solid support unit, often equipped with a baseball bat for increased stats.

The Tower meta is constantly shifting, especially with major updates. Every 100 floors guarantees a Sid, and there's a 5% chance for a shiny unit. While the current meta heavily involves RNG, strategic team building and adapting to enemy compositions are vital. For example, facing an enemy Itachi means your units might take significant damage from his counterattack, while an enemy Eisen can stun your key units. The *Anime Story 2 re-release* will likely introduce new top-tier units, making the Tower an exciting, albeit unpredictable, challenge.
