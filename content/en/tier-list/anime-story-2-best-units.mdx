## Best Units for Story Mode and Early Farming

When you first start your journey, your primary goal is to clear the story maps as quickly as possible to unlock better rewards and higher difficulty tiers. For this purpose, Area of Effect (AoE) damage is king. One of the **anime story 2 best units** for beginners is undoubtedly **Ace**. He is remarkably easy to build and can carry a player through several maps, potentially reaching as far as Soul Haven single-handedly.

To maximize Ace's potential, players often equip him with the Baseball Bat relic and aim for Triple S stats. According to community reports, achieving a guaranteed Triple S on all stats requires approximately 6,000 to 6,500 takedowns. Once Ace is fully built with high attack and crit stats, he can one-shot most mobs in the early to mid-game maps.

If you are lucky enough to pull **Beiru** from a secret orb or through codes, your progression will skyrocket. For the late-game story maps, such as the final map currently available, units like **Tengen** or **Rimuru** are the preferred choices. These units provide the perfect balance of XP, gold, and gems, making them the most efficient farmers for players looking to stockpile resources for the next big update.

## Raid Mastery: Units for Maximum DPS

Raids require a different approach than story mode. Instead of clearing waves of enemies, you are focused on burning down a high-HP boss as quickly as possible. High single-target DPS and status effects are the keys to success here. **Broly Rookie** is frequently cited as a top-tier choice because of his raw damage output, even when he doesn't have a direct elemental advantage.

However, many veteran players consider **Eisen** to be one of the absolute **anime story 2 best units** for raid content. Eisen's ability to stun the boss is invaluable, often allowing your team to bypass dangerous mechanics entirely. When paired with a unit like **Beiru**, who provides a 10% damage buff and applies Black Flames for additional damage over time, the raid bosses melt in seconds.

Another effective strategy involves the combination of **Beiru and Gojo**. Gojo's ability to double damage over time once poison effects are applied can result in boss kills within 6 to 8 seconds. This "poison meta" is highly effective for players who may not have the highest raw attack stats but want to utilize status-based synergies to clear content quickly.

## The Slime Apocalypse: The Perfect Farming Comp

The Slime Apocalypse event requires a very specific setup to achieve the fastest clear times. Based on community strategies shared on Discord, particularly by a prominent player named Acid, there is a "perfect" team composition that consistently clears the event in under 10 seconds. This team relies on a mix of elemental damage and specific level-gating mechanics.

*   **Shiny Ultimate Warrior:** A must-have for this mode, as it deals 2x more damage when attacking with elemental damage (specifically water for the current era).
*   **Bond:** This unit is essential for his execution passive, which one-shots any enemy under 25% HP.
*   **Cyborg (Level 193):** This is a highly specific "pro tip." At level 193, Cyborg has the perfect balance of health and damage to be one-shot by the enemy, triggering his death explosion which deals massive damage to all remaining foes. If he is too high level, he won't die quickly enough, ruining the speed run.
*   **Tatsumaki:** Used primarily for her 30% damage buff to the rest of the team.
*   **Broly Rookie:** Acts as a tank to soak up initial stuns or attacks, ensuring your DPS units can fire off their abilities.

Using this specific setup, players have reported farming over 70,000 gems in a relatively short period. It is currently the most consistent way to accumulate wealth in the game, provided you have the units and have kept your Cyborg at the specific level required for the strategy to work.

## Tower Mode: Climbing the Leaderboards

The Tower is arguably the most challenging content in *Anime Story 2*, and at the highest floors, the meta revolves heavily around RNG (Random Number Generation). To reach the top 100, you need a team that can survive massive hits while dealing enough damage to clear enemies with millions of HP.

**Eisen** remains a staple here because he stuns three enemies at the start of a round for one turn. This is arguably the most broken mechanic in the game, as it prevents the enemy team from wiping you instantly. **Itachi** is another top-tier choice for the Tower due to his 25% chance to avoid damage and counterattack. His passive makes him incredibly difficult to kill, often buying your team the extra turn needed to secure a victory.

For those looking for high-risk, high-reward plays, **Sound Breather** is a controversial but effective pick. While he is often described as "suicidal" due to his mechanics, if he lands his full combo, he can decimate an entire enemy lineup in a single turn. When building your Tower team, don't forget the importance of relics. The Baseball Bat is generally considered the best-in-slot item for almost every unit except Itachi, who benefits more from raw HP to survive the hits he doesn't dodge.

## Essential Traits and Stat Priorities

Beyond just choosing the right characters, you must optimize their traits and stats to truly consider them among the **anime story 2 best units**. For most DPS units, the "Dead Eye" trait is highly sought after, especially if you are running a crit-focused relic build. 

When it comes to stats, the priority is almost always Attack > Crit Damage > Crit Range. While Health is important for Tower units like Itachi, most other modes favor a "glass cannon" approach where you kill the enemy before they have a chance to strike. Remember that grinding for Triple S stats is a long process, but it is the only way to maximize the potential of your secret and shiny units.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who are the anime story 2 best units for beginners?**
A: Ace is widely considered the best beginner unit due to his full AoE attacks and ease of building. Equipping him with a Baseball Bat relic and grinding for Triple S stats can carry you through the majority of the early story maps.

**Q: Why is Cyborg kept at level 193 for Slime Apocalypse?**
A: This is a community-discovered strategy. At level 193, Cyborg has low enough health to be killed instantly by the slimes, which triggers his "detonate upon death" passive. This deals massive damage to all enemies, allowing for a 9-11 second clear time.

**Q: Is RNG really that important in the Tower?**
A: Yes. At higher floors, even the **anime story 2 best units** like Eisen and Itachi rely on RNG. Success often depends on whether Eisen's stun lands on the correct enemies or whether Itachi successfully dodges and counterattacks.

**Q: What is the best way to get Triple S stats on a unit?**
A: To get a guaranteed Triple S on all stats, you generally need to reach between 6,000 and 6,500 takedowns with that specific unit. This is most easily done on early story maps with high enemy density.
