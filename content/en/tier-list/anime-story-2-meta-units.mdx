## Mastering Story Mode: Top Anime Story 2 Meta Units for Campaign Progress

Story mode is your foundation in *Anime Story 2*, providing essential resources and unlocking new content. Choosing the right units here can drastically speed up your progression. For early and mid-game story chapters, efficiency and AoE damage are key.

One standout unit for Story Mode, especially for beginners, is **Ace**. He's relatively easy to build and boasts full AoE capabilities, making him excellent for clearing waves of enemies quickly. Players report that a well-invested Ace, particularly with triple S stats in attack and potentially crit damage (depending on traits like 'Dead Eye' and relic usage), can carry you through a significant portion of the story, potentially up to Soul Haven. Achieving guaranteed triple S stats on Ace is achievable with around 6,000 to 6,500 takedowns.

As you progress, other **Anime Story 2 meta units** become viable, particularly for farming the later stages. If you manage to acquire **Beiru** from a secret orb (after popping all codes), he can extend your reach even further. For the absolute last map, which is ideal for farming XP, gold, and gems, units like **Ten** or **Remu** are highly recommended. Another strong contender for this stage is **Tempest**, offering solid performance.

For those looking for a quick gem farming method, a community-reported "bug" in Act 3, Nightmare difficulty, involving double Genos, can yield fast gem returns (2.3-2.5 seconds per clear). However, this method sacrifices gold and XP, so it's best for players solely focused on gems. Ultimately, for Story mode, prioritize full AoE units or specific high-efficiency characters like Sid for map 93.

## Dominating Raids: Best Units for High Damage Output

Raids in *Anime Story 2* demand high single-target damage and specific utility to quickly dispatch powerful bosses. While starting raids can be tricky, certain **Anime Story 2 meta units** make the process significantly smoother.

**Broly Rookie** is a strong recommendation for raids. Despite not always having a full elemental advantage, his raw damage output is substantial. For players aiming to optimize their Slime team, acquiring **Monarch** is crucial, and he also performs well in general raids. However, the community widely agrees that the undisputed best unit for farming raids is **Eisen** himself. His ability to stun enemies on round start for one turn, combined with a 200% damage bonus on his first attack, makes him incredibly potent.

Another effective raid team composition includes **Beiru** and **Gojo**. Notably, Beiru does not require Monarch for this setup. Gojo's ability to double damage over time, especially with poisons, allows for quick boss takedowns (around 6-8 seconds). Essentially, any high single-target DPS unit or your strongest damage dealer should be prioritized for raid farming.

For maximum efficiency, especially for Act 6 raids, an Eisen-centric strategy is often employed. Pairing Eisen with Beiru can make runs much easier, as Beiru provides additional support. While Eisen is incredibly powerful, players should be mindful of his susceptibility to being stunned, which can sometimes disrupt optimal runs. For subsequent raids, Eisen continues to be a top-tier choice, capable of clearing content with relative ease, though his one-shot potential might vary without full support. Always ensure your unit stats are optimized for damage for the best results.

## Slime Apocalypse Event: The Ultimate Farming Team

The Slime Apocalypse event is a fantastic source of resources, and having an optimized team is key to consistent, fast clears. The community-tested meta team for this event, credited to "Acid" on Discord, focuses on specific synergies and strategic unit placement.

The core of this team revolves around **Shiny Ultimate Warrior**, a must-have due to its 2x damage bonus when attacking with elemental damage (water in this event). **Bond** is another crucial component, as he can one-shot enemies below 25% HP.

The most unique and strategic unit in this composition is **Cyborg**. Cyborg deals massive damage to all enemies upon death. The trick is to have two Cyborgs at a specific level (around 193) where they have enough damage and health to die quickly, triggering their massive AoE damage, but not so much health that they survive and ruin the run. If they are too high level, they won't die immediately, breaking the strategy.

Rounding out the team are **Tatsumiki** for a 30% damage boost and **Rookie** to tank incoming attacks and stuns. The sequence typically involves Broly one-shotting early enemies, Rookie taking hits, and then the Cyborgs detonating to clear the remaining slimes, leading to consistent clear times often between 9-12 seconds. This setup is highly effective for rapid farming, as demonstrated by players accumulating vast amounts of currency.

## Conquering the Tower: RNG and Meta Unit Strategies

The Tower in *Anime Story 2* is a challenging, RNG-heavy game mode where team composition and strategic unit choice are paramount. While the meta can shift with updates, certain **Anime Story 2 meta units** remain consistently strong. As of early 2026, the Tower meta often favors units with disruptive abilities and high damage.

**Eisen** is considered a must-have. His ability to stun three enemies on round start for one turn is incredibly powerful, often allowing your team to gain an early advantage. The 200% damage bonus on his first attack further solidifies his position, enabling potential one-shots against weaker foes.

**Itachi** is another essential unit due to his defensive and counter-attacking capabilities. He has a 25% chance to avoid damage and counterattack for 50% damage when hit. Furthermore, after being hit, the next attack against Itachi misses, introducing a significant element of RNG-based survivability.

**Cyborg** can be a controversial but effective pick. If the enemy team has AoE units that don't instantly wipe your squad, Cyborg can die, explode, and clear the enemy team, allowing your remaining units to finish. However, if your entire team is wiped, it's still a loss. **Sound Breather** is another high-risk, high-reward unit; if his combo lands, he can decimate enemies, but his suicidal nature makes him unreliable without proper setup.

**Slime** is also a strong choice, providing a 10% damage buff to your team and allowing black flames to deal 66% damage, increasing enemy damage taken by 10%. Pairing these with **Beiru**, often equipped with a baseball bat (except for Itachi, who benefits from higher HP to trigger his passive), forms a potent Tower team.

The Tower meta is heavily influenced by RNG. Facing enemies like Itachi, Eisen, or Igoras, whose passive abilities also apply, requires careful team adjustments. For example, an enemy Eisen can stun your key units, potentially disrupting your strategy. Units like **Ten** and **Tempest** can also be powerful DPS options, capable of clearing significant portions of enemy HP. Ultimately, success in the Tower often boils down to adapting your team to the specific enemy lineup and hoping for favorable RNG.
