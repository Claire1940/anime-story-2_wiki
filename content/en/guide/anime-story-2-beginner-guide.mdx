### Conquering Story Mode: Your First Steps to Power

The Story Mode in Anime Story 2 is your primary source of early-game resources, experience, and the foundation for unlocking more advanced content. Building an efficient team for story progression is crucial for any new player.

#### Recommended Units for Early Story Progression

*   **Ace:** This unit is a phenomenal choice for beginners due to his full AoE (Area of Effect) capabilities, making him incredibly easy to farm for triple S stats. Focus on maximizing his attack, critical range, and critical damage. With enough takedowns (around 6,000-6,500), you can guarantee triple S on his stats, allowing Ace to carry you through many early stages, potentially even up to Soul Haven.
*   **Beiru & Ten (from Secret Orbs):** If you're lucky enough to pull Beiru from a secret orb and utilize all available codes, your story progression can accelerate significantly. Beiru, combined with Ace, can help clear stages like the one that yields 270 gems per run. Similarly, if you acquire Ten, farming the last map for all-in-one XP, gold, and gems becomes much easier.
*   **Tempest:** Another strong unit for story mode, Tempest offers excellent damage output that can help you clear stages efficiently, especially in Nightmare difficulty.
*   **Farming Strategy:** For optimal gem farming, especially if you're not yet at Floor 100 in the Tower, consider Act 3's Nightmare stage with double Genos. While it might slightly reduce gold and XP compared to the last story stage, it's known to be one of the fastest ways to acquire gems. For overall resources, the last story map (with Ten or Remu) is ideal.

Remember, the key to story mode success is often having strong AoE units that can clear waves quickly. Prioritize units like Ace early on, and as you acquire more diverse characters, adapt your team to the specific challenges of each act.

### Raiding for Riches: Essential Units and Strategies

Raids are an excellent way to farm specific items and units in Anime Story 2, but they require a more focused approach with single-target damage dealers.

#### Top Units for Raid Farming

*   **Broly (Rookie):** Broly Rookie is a solid choice for raids. Despite not always having a type advantage, his raw damage output is substantial. He's also a valuable unit for the Slime Apocalypse team.
*   **Eisen:** Hands down, Eisen is often considered the best unit for farming raids. His ability to stun bosses and deal massive damage makes many raid encounters trivial. Equipping him with a baseball bat (which boosts damage) further enhances his effectiveness. With Eisen, you can often solo or quickly clear raid bosses, especially in Act 6, due to his stun locking capabilities.
*   **Beiru & Gojo:** This duo forms another effective raid team. Beiru, especially when paired with Gojo (who doubles damage over time from poisons), can quickly melt raid bosses in about 6-8 seconds once the poison effects kick in. Notably, Beiru doesn't require Monarch for this specific build.

#### Raid Team Composition Tips

For raids, your primary goal is high single-target DPS. Units that can stun, apply debuffs, or simply hit incredibly hard are your best bet. Always aim to bring your strongest single-target attackers. As a new player, focusing on getting and leveling up units like Eisen will significantly boost your raid farming efficiency.

### Dominating the Slime Apocalypse Event

The Slime Apocalypse is a unique event that requires a very specific team composition to achieve consistent and fast clears, essential for efficient farming of event-specific rewards. This setup, credited to Discord user "Acid," is highly effective.

#### Optimal Slime Apocalypse Team

*   **Shiny Ultimate Warrior:** A must-have unit for this event. He deals 2x more damage when attacking with elemental damage, which is crucial as the enemies in this event are water-elemental.
*   **Bond:** Bond is vital because he one-shots enemies under 25% HP, helping to clean up the remaining slimes quickly.
*   **Cyborg (x2):** This is where it gets interesting. Cyborg is a "fun" unit because upon death, he detonates, dealing massive damage to all enemies. The trick is to have two Cyborgs at a specific level (around 193) where they have enough damage and health to get one-shot by the slimes but still deal enough damage to clear the AoE units. Leveling them too high will prevent them from dying, ruining the strategy.
*   **Tatsumiki:** Provides a significant 30% damage boost to your team, further enhancing the clear speed.
*   **Rookie:** Acts as a tank, absorbing attacks and stuns, protecting your more fragile damage dealers.

#### How the Slime Apocalypse Team Works

The strategy involves Broly (usually the first attacker) taking out the initial wave, followed by Rookie tanking, and then the two Cyborgs dying and exploding to clear out the AoE units. Tatsumiki boosts overall damage, and Bond finishes off any remaining low-HP enemies. This setup consistently yields fast clear times (around 9-12 seconds), making it one of the most efficient farming methods in the game for event currencies and other rewards. This **Anime Story 2 beginner guide** emphasizes the importance of specific unit levels for optimal performance in this event.

### Ascending the Tower: A Test of RNG and Strategy

The Tower in Anime Story 2 is a challenging mode that rewards progression with valuable units like Sid and a chance at shiny variants every 100 floors. The current meta, however, heavily relies on RNG, making team composition and strategic unit placement crucial.

#### Essential Tower Units (RNG-Dependent)

*   **Eisen:** A definite must-have. He stuns three enemies at the start of a round for one turn and deals 200% damage on his first attack. This can often one-shot weaker enemies or significantly cripple stronger ones.
*   **Itachi:** Another must-have. He has a 25% chance to avoid damage and counterattack for 50% damage when hit. After being hit, the next attack against Itachi misses. This passive introduces a significant RNG element but can be game-changing.
*   **Cyborg:** Controversial but potentially useful. If the enemy team has AoE units that don't one-shot your entire team, a dying Cyborg can detonate and clear their units, allowing your remaining units to finish the job. However, if the entire team dies, it's still a loss.
*   **Sound Breather:** A high-risk, high-reward unit. If he lands his combo, he can decimate enemies, but his effectiveness is highly dependent on RNG.
*   **Slime:** Provides a 10% damage buff and his Black Flames deal 66% damage, which can be exchanged to make enemies take 10% more damage. This support unit is invaluable for boosting your team's overall output.
*   **Beiru:** Often paired with a baseball bat (to maximize HP and ensure he's targeted), Beiru can be a strong addition to a tower team, especially if you need consistent damage or specific utility.
*   **Igoras:** A powerful unit that, once he starts rolling, can quickly wipe out enemy teams. However, facing an enemy Igoras can be devastating.

#### Tower Strategy: Embracing the RNG

The Tower is largely an RNG fest. Your success often hinges on lucky stuns from Eisen, Itachi's evasion, or Sound Breather's combos. Team composition often needs to be adjusted based on the enemy lineup. For instance, facing enemy Itachis can be problematic due to their passive. Similarly, double Eisen or an Igoras on the enemy side requires careful planning and a bit of prayer. While there might not be one "perfect" team for every stage, understanding your units' capabilities and adapting them to the enemy's strengths is key. Don't be afraid to experiment with different lineups and unit placements.

### Optimizing Your Grind: General Tips for Beginners

*   **Stat Allocation:** For damage dealers like Ace, prioritize Attack, Crit Range, and Crit Damage. For tanks or utility units, focus on what enhances their specific role.
*   **Relics:** Don't overlook the power of relics. They can significantly boost your units' stats and abilities. For example, a crit-focused relic can make units with traits like "Dead Eye" even more potent.
*   **Events are Key:** Participate in all available events. The Slime Apocalypse, for instance, offers a fantastic opportunity to farm resources efficiently with the right team.
*   **Community Resources:** Join the official Anime Story 2 Discord server. ### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What's the best unit for a new player to focus on in Anime Story 2?**
A: Ace is an excellent starting unit due to his full AoE damage and ease of acquiring triple S stats, making him perfect for early story progression.

**Q: How can I efficiently farm gems in Anime Story 2?**
A: For fast gem farming, consider the Nightmare stage in Act 3 with double Genos. While it might sacrifice some gold and XP, it's known for quick gem acquisition. The last story map also offers a good balance of XP, gold, and gems.

**Q: What units are essential for raiding in Anime Story 2?**
A: Eisen is arguably the best unit for raids due to his stun and high damage. Broly (Rookie) and the Beiru/Gojo combination are also highly effective single-target damage dealers for raid bosses.

**Q: Is the Tower mode in Anime Story 2 purely RNG-based?**
A: While strategy and unit composition are important, the Tower mode does have a significant RNG element, relying on unit passives like Eisen's stun and Itachi's evasion. Adapting your team to enemy lineups and hoping for favorable outcomes is part of the challenge.
